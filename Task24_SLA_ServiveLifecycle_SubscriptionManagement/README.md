# Task 24: SLA, Service Lifecycle & Subscription Management (Easy)

## Objective
The goal of this task is to **explore Service Level Agreements (SLA), service lifecycle, and subscription management in Azure**. This allows understanding governance, planning, and cost considerations without creating paid resources.

## Steps Performed

### Step 1: Explore SLA Information
1. Open **Azure Portal → Search bar → type “SLA” → click Service Level Agreements**  
2. Browse through **Azure services** and their SLA percentages (e.g., Storage, Virtual Machines, App Service)  
3. Take note of SLA details, such as:
   - Uptime guarantees  
   - Downtime allowances  
   - SLA exceptions  

> This demonstrates understanding of **service reliability and planning**.

---

### Step 2: Explore Service Lifecycle
1. Open **Azure Portal → Search bar → type “Service Lifecycle” → click Azure Service Lifecycle**  
2. Review information about **services entering preview, general availability, and retirement**  
3. Observe which services are in **preview or generally available (GA)**  

> This shows awareness of **service lifecycle management**.

---

### Step 3: Manage Subscriptions
1. Go to **Azure Portal → Subscriptions**  
2. View your subscription(s), e.g., Pay-As-You-Go, Free Trial  
3. Observe details such as:
   - Subscription name and ID  
   - Billing scope  
   - Directory association  
   - Spending limits or quotas  
4. Optional: Click **Overview** → check subscription status  

## Outcome
- Explored SLA information for Azure services  
- Reviewed Azure service lifecycle stages  
- Checked subscription details, including billing scope and status  
- Took required screenshots as proof
